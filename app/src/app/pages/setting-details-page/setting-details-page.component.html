Randomizer Settings Details

Shuffle Items

  • All freestanding items on the overworld.
  • All items, badges and key items located in ? blocks.
  • All items found in trees and bushes, except the ones part of Koopa Koot's quests.
  • Most rewards handed / traded by NPCs. Currently excluded: Russ T. reward for bringing the dictionary, post office Mailbag reward.
  • Rip Cheato's trading items are randomized in this setting, he is not considered as a shop.
  • The anti-guy chest in Toy Box can contain a progression item. However, the logic doesn't expect you to beat him, as he can be bribed with a Lemon Candy. This means the logic expects you to have access to Cookbook, cake mix and lemon before placing a progression item there.

Shopsanity

  • Melow's badge shop has random items for sale. The first 3 ones cost 2SP, the next 3 cost 3SP...And so on until the last 3 for 6SP.
  • The logic considers the player needs 60 star pieces for any of Merlow's items to be mandatory, to prevent being softlocked by buying all the useless items.
  • Rowf's badge shop now sells random items and he upgrades his inventory after each star spirit saved. He updates his inventory 5 times total.
  • Rowf's items are all unique. Items sold by regular shops can be bought infinitely if they are consumables. Badges and key items can only be bought once.

Coinsanity

  • All freestanding coins on the overworld.
  • All coins located in ? blocks.
  • Coins in bushes and trees are not included.

Include Hidden Panels

  • The Ultra Hammer OR Super Boots is required to flip panels.
  • Logic doesn't consider it required to have the ISpy badge to detect panels, so it is highly recommended you use the Always ISpy setting if you're not familiar with the locations.

Keysanity

  • Items affected by this setting: Ruins Key, Pyramid Stone, Lunar Stone, Diamond Stone, Castle Key, Toy Train, Dictionary, Mystery Note, Cake, Magical Bean, Fertile Soil, Miracle Water, Bubble Berry, Red Berry, Blue Berry, Yellow Berry, Blue Key, Red Key, Palace Key.

Include Dojo Rewards

  • The logic can only expect you to do the 2nd fight with 2 star spirits saved, 3rd fight with 3 star spirits saved, and so on.

Shuffle Partners

  • Starting partners will already have their "quest" considered done and you won't be able to talk to them and get an item from them.
  • An important implication of this is that when starting with Bow, the mansion's shop and Gusty Gulch gate will be opened right away, since the vanilla requirement to open them is to get Bow at the top of the mansion.
  • Items that are part of the partner quest (Kooper's Shell, Boo portrait) will be removed from the item pool when starting with that partner.

Start With Random Partners

  • When setting a wide range of min/max number of partners, it is way more likely to get a low number.
  • The algorithm starts by giving you the lowest number of partners, then flips a coin for every following partner.
  • Example: Min 1, Max 8. You'll have a 50% chance to get 2 partners, 25% to get 3, 12.5% to get 4.

Shuffle BP, FP, SP options

  • The values are only shuffled between what's in the game for that category. This means that badges with a high BP requirement are almost certain to have it lowered, for example.
  • Badgew descriptionw are not updated, so the required amount of FP for badge abilities in the badge menu's description won't be accurate with Shuffle Badges FP

Color options

  • Random pick means the color is determined during seed generation and will stay constant throughout gameplay
  • Random on every load means the color will be picked at random ingame, and might change at every loading
  • Random pick includes the default palette so it is possible to see no change if the default is randomly picked
 Difficulty Settings Details

Enemy Difficulty

  • Progressive Scaling gets harder according to the number of progression key items and partners you get
  • Partners give 3x more progress score than key items
  • If Mario's Star Power is greater than the current difficulty level determined by the progress score, star power will be taken as chapter difficulty instead. For example, if you have 3 star power gauges but a low progress score that would only compute as chapter 3 difficulty, chapter 4 difficulty would be taken
  • Random Chapter Difficulty has no logic, so it might be possible that you are required to beat very hard fights early on.

Item Scarcity - Difficulty Levels

  • Items have been separated in 10 tiers according to how strong they are considered
  • When the generator places a non-essential item, it has a chance to replace it with another from a lower tier according to the setting's value:
  • 0: Items are never replaced
  • 1: 30% chance to divide tier by 2
  • 2: 40% chance to divide tier by 2, 10% chance to divide tier by 3
  • 3: 50% chance to divide tier by 2, 20% chance to divide tier by 3
  • 4: 60% chance to divide tier by 2, 40% chance to divide tier by 3
  • 5: 100% chance to divide tier by 3

 Open World Settings Details

Starting Location

  • Choosing Dry Dry Outpost as a starting location forces the Homeward Shroom option ON. This is because it is possible to head to Toad Town from the desert, but impossible to come back without Parakarry.

Homeward Shroom

  • Warps Mario back to the starting location
  • Cannot be used during the volcano escape and Tubba Blubba's Castle Escape
 Quality of Life Settings Details

Skip Quiz

  • Quizmo will instantly give a star piece when talked to. Keep in mind that without this setting, you can get up to 8 star pieces in a row by answering questions right though.

Shorten Cutscenes

  • Currently applies to: First Mt.Rugged train ride, Merluvlee's fortune telling, ch6. magic bean growing, star ship ride to Bowser's Castle.
  • More shortened cutscenes will be added over time.

Shorten Bowser's Castle

  • The front door is already unlocked.
  • The entire undeground section has been cut. The first Bowser guard door will actually be the second, asking quiz questions.
  • The torches puzzle labyrinth room has been removed.
  • The water drain rooms where you need Sushie have been removed.
  • As a result, no key is required to reach Peach's Castle
